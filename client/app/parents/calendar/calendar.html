Events Calendar


January, 2015

  • Parent-Teacher Conferences – Early dismissal for 4 days while teachers meet with parents by request only

February

  • 100’s Day – On the 100th day of school, students bring in a collection of 100 objects to count and share. Each class enjoys a range of activities that vary each year, and some time with their “buddies” to explore this important milestone number.
  • Valentine’s Day – students bring a valentine for each child in their class
  • Mid-Winter Recess – one week break beginning on President’s Day

March

  • LEAP’s Book Fair (District wide) – The first week in March, LEAP hosts a Scholastic Book Fair. Children visit with their classes and parents are invited to purchase books for home, gifts, summer reading, and for teachers’ classroom wish lists. In addition to raising funds for our campus and classroom libraries, we generate excitement around books and reading.
  • Spring Equinox – At 9:00 on the Friday closest to the Equinox (March 22) each class shares a presentation in the Montessori Amphitheater. Songs, poetry, skits, and lots of information about the Equinox inform and delight parents and students. After the presentations, there is a field trip to beautiful Divide Meadow in Point Reyes where we hike, picnic, play, and explore our natural world and celebrate signs of the coming spring.
  • LEAP’s Read-A-Thon (District wide)– The entire month of March the entire district participates in a double-goal fundraiser where kids collect sponsorships and keep a log (much like summer library reading programs) of their reading. Silent independent reading, being read to before bed, listening to an audiobook, reading to a younger sibling or parent, and reading in class all count! This raises money and helps encourage reading.

April

  • Spring Recess – one week break
  • Parent-Teacher Conferences – Early dismissal for 4 days while teachers meet with parents by request only

May

  • Valley Visions – (First Saturday of May) This is THE major LEAP fundraiser for the school district and Montessori parents participate with parents from the entire district to guarantee a fun and successful community event.
  • Outdoor Education Trip (4th & 5th Grade class) alternates each year between: Odd years: Environmental Overnight trip to the Marin Headlands/YMCA Camp. Even years: Gold Country Trip –– As a part of the fourth/fifth grade California study, the older students participate in an overnight trip to Malakoff Diggins where they re-enact the California Gold Rush days.
  • Montessori Open House/Spring Sing - Meet in the Multi-Purpose Room for a musical performance showcasing what each class is studying in music class and band. Then, visit your child’s classroom where your child will love showing you around and teachers will answer any questions. You will see the wonderful projects where the Cultural Studies come alive along with their work with math, spelling, writing samples, and more.

June

  • Montessori All Program Continent Celebration Day . Much of our curriculum and many activities revolve around the continent studies which are explained in the Montessori Curriculum Guide.
  • End of year trip to Heart’s Desire Beach - Picnic, explore, hike, and swim to celebrate another great year together.
  • Last Student Day of the School Year –– Kindergarten is a big transition and we like to mark the transition to the full-day grades with a sweet little Kindergarten Graduation ceremony in the classroom to celebrate this big step.
  • Montessori 5th Grade Graduation takes place at 11 a.m. on the courtyard stage. Music, poetry, speeches, family, friends, and the wider school community are all included to show our great pride in each student’s unique gifts, accomplishments, and learning in the Montessori program a wish them success in Middle School and beyond!
