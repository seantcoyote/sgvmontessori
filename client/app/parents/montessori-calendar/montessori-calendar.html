Events Calendar

2015-2016 Schoolyear

August

  • Welcome to Montessori Potluck — This is a potluck to welcome new students and their families to the Montessori Program. New and veteran parents, caregivers and kids are welcome!

September

  • The 5 Great Lessons — All Montessori teachers gather the program and present the Montessori tradition of the 5 great lesson: from the creation of the universe to the creation of numbers and society.
  • Back to School Night — Parents are invited to their children's' classrooms to meet the teachers and learn about classroom activities
  • Camping Trip — PTO sponsored full program camping trip with potluck dinners, nighttime nature walks, and campfire fun! Camping is Friday and Saturday nights and campsite is usually local at Pt Reyes or Tamarancho campground.
  • Autumn equinox — Students give presentations celebrating the equinox- songs, poems, and skits are all popular — to parents and the other program students in the Montessori amphitheater before heading to Heart's Desire Beach for a day of picnicking, socializing, and beach fun.

October

  • Montessori Program's Fall Festival Fundraiser — Our program's big fundraiser for the year! A fun fall family event with live music, games, cake walk, raffle, seasonal crafts, and delicious homemade food and treats.
  • Parent-Teacher Conferences — Early dismissal for 4 days while teachers meet with each child's parents for a conference.
  • Montessori Historical Halloween — Students take their place on the timeline, showing that all characters- real and imagined- belong to a place in time. Students then join together for a costumed parade and LEAP's district-wide Halloween Carnival.

November

  • The Montessori Thanksgiving Feast — near Thanksgiving break we gather together as a program to eat, sing songs, and celebrate everything for which we we feel grateful.

December

  • Winter Recess — 2 week school break.

January

  • Parent-Teacher Conferences — Early dismissal for 4 days while teachers meet with parents by request only. Teachers are preparing progress reports at this time as well.

February

  • 100's Day — On the 100th day of school, students bring in a collection of 100 objects to count and share. Each class enjoys a range of activities that vary each year, and some time with their "buddies" to explore this important milestone number.
  • Valentine's Day — students bring a valentine for each child in their class.
  • Mid-Winter Recess — one week break around President's Day.

March

  • LEAP's Book Fair (Districtwide) — The first week in March, LEAP hosts a Scholastic Book Fair. Children visit with their classes and parents are invited to purchase books for home, gifts, summer reading, and for teachers' classroom wish lists. In addition to raising funds for our campus and classroom libraries, we generate excitement around books and reading.
  • Spring Equinox — At 9:00 on the Friday closest to the equinox (March 22), each class shares a presentation in the Montessori Amphitheater. Songs, poetry, skits, and lots of information about the Equinox inform and delight parents and students. After the presentations, there is a field trip to beautiful Divide Meadow in Point Reyes where we hike, picnic, play, and explore our natural world and celebrate signs of the coming spring.
  • LEAP's Read-A-Thon (District wide) — The month of March, the entire district participates in a double-goal fundraiser where kids collect sponsorships and keep a log of their reading (much like summer library reading programs). Silent, independent reading, being read to before bed, listening to an audiobook, reading to a younger sibling or parent, and reading in class all count! This raises money and helps encourage reading.

April

  • Spring Recess — one week break.
  • Parent-Teacher Conferences — Early dismissal for 4 days while teachers meet with parents by request only. Teachers are preparing progress reports at this time as well.

May

  • Valley Visions (First Saturday of May) — This is THE major LEAP fundraiser for the school district and Montessori parents participate with parents from the entire district to guarantee a fun and successful community event.
  • Outdoor Education Trip (4th & 5th Grade class) — alternates each year between: Odd years — environmental overnight trip to the Marin Headlands/YMCA Camp. Even years: GOld Country Trip — as part of the fourth/fifth grade California study, the older students participate in an overnight trip to Malakoff Diggins, where they re-enact the California Gold rush days.
  • Montessori Open House/Spring Sing — Meet in the Multi-Purpose Room for a musical performance showcasing what each class is studying in music class and band. Then, visit your child's classroom where your child will love showing you around and teachers will answer questions.

June

  • Montessori All Program Continent Celebration Day — much of our curriculum and many activities revolve around the continent studies which are explained in the Montessori Curriculum Guide.
  • End of year trip to Heart's Desire Beach — Picnic, explore, hike, and swim to celebrate another great year together.
  • Last Student Day of the School Year — Kindergarten is a big transition and we like to mark the transition to the full-day grades with a sweet little Kindergarten Graduation ceremony in the classroom to celebrate this big step.
  • Montessori 5th Grade Graduation takes place at 11 a.m. on the courtyard stage. Music, poetry, speeches, family, friends, and the wider school community are all included to show our great pride in each student's unique gifts, accomplishments, and learning in the Montessori program as we wish them success in Middle School and beyond!
